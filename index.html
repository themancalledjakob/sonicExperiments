Sound Design lesson 0 - 03.02.2016

pre - assignment

I removed the sound of three movie scenes for you:
- Tuttle's End (Brazil)
- Cropduster (North By Northwest)
- Arthur Zanetti Artistic Rings Gold (Olympics 2012)

My lesson for you is to make a soundscape for these movie scenes, and try to change how they feel.

Instructions:
- pic two of the movie scenes (north by north west, brazil or olympics)
- download them here: http://we.tl/qD90oTNWJI
- rent an audiorecorder for today (when you say it's for the course you don't have to pay. otherwise you'll get it back next week)
- record sound and noises around the academy that you think fit to the atmosphere of the scene.
- put your recordings under the scenes and bring them next week.

Important is here more the atmospheric fit, and NOT the editing.
Keep the recordings as raw as possible. You should think about how you can influence or control the perception of the scenes, and how much power the sound gives you here. It does not have to be perfect. Your recordings will probably be rough, use that for expression!

That's it :-)

We'll discuss your recordings next week then when I'm there, but let me know if you have any questions!

Have fun!

Sound Design lesson I - 10.02.2016

assignment:

If you were there on wednesday, please ignore the first steps:
1. write 3 adjectives each on a piece of paper
2. pick one of the adjectives blindly
3. make a soundpiece between 30 seconds and 3 minutes to introduce yourself and try to use the adjective as a theme (people should be able to guess the adjective)

I know this is hard! If you can't identify yourself easily with the adjective, try to find a memory or personal story that fits.
